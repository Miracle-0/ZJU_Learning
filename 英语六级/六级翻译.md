Since ancient times, China's water resources have been unevenly distributed, with the north lacking water while the south having an abundance. 

To effectively address the severe water shortage in the north, the Chinese government launched the South-to-North Water Diversion Project, a massive water conservancy project allocating water resources across different regions. 

The project, which started construction in 2002, consists of three routes—eastern, central, and western—covering a total length of 4,350 kilometers and benefiting over 400 million people. 

Since it began supplying water in 2014, the project has transferred over 50 billion cubic meters of water to the north. 

It has not only provided a reliable water supply for people in the north, but also significantly improved the local ecological environment, promoting sustainable and healthy economic development.
